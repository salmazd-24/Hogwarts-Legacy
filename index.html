Freely roam Hogwarts, Hogsmeade, the Forbidden Forest, and the surrounding Overland area. Learn spells, brew potions, grow plants, and tend to magical beasts along your journey. Companions

Companions in Hogwarts Legacy are fellow students of the magic academy, that may join you during certain sections of the game. Each Companion has its own personality, as well as its own magical arsenal and preferred combat style. They may even assist you during some combat challenges!

Poppy Sweeting

Poppy is a bright and compassionate girl with extensive knowledge of and a deep love for creatures.

Natsai Onai

Natsai grew up in Matabeleland, Africa where she attended Uagadou. She transferred to Hogwarts when her mother became the school's Divination professor and is adapting to her new life in Scotland.

Sebastian Sallow

Sebastian is a channing and extroverted boy who convinced that his cursed sister last hope for a cure lies in the Dark Arts - and he is determined to find it.

Tips 1: Use Revelio all the time to reveal hidden things and earn XP faster

Revelio shows danger, loot, interactable stuff and anything you might need or want. Use it all the time to make sure you don't miss out on anything you could otherwise walk past.

Tips 2: Check gear constantly to swap in higher level stuff and improve stats as you collect it

Looking regularly and making sure improving your characters with an extra point. If you see a green arrow next to an item, it means it has better stats so, as soon as you can, swap things out.

Tips 3: Increase your gear inventory by completing Merlin Trials mission

When you first start the game, you will have 20 gear slots available. Unfortunately, once your gear slots are full you'll need to remove items or sell them. Because of this, we recommend you to finding and completing Merlin Trials across the map.

Tips 4: Eye chests are a quick way to get fast cash

Hogwarts Legacy eye chests appear early in the game. Initially they'll slam shut the second you get near them, but opening them will earn you 500 Galleons.

Did you know?
